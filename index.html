I'm a second year Computer Science / Computer Engineering student at USC. Currently, I work as an intern with Dr. John Heidemann at the ANT Lab in Marina Del Rey, where I pursue my interests in the fields of network security and data analysis.

Research

I'm working on the Retrofuture Project, where I conduct research on data anonymization techniques and create / fiddle with packet capture and data visualization tools. This summer, I'm helping with a paper on data anonymization and organizationally-aware data-sharing practices at the Los Alamos National Laboratories. Recent work includes:

  • A data visualization tool for tracking /24 block outages in ISI data. Completed for the Trinocular Project.Was but a frosh, learning to love in a lonely world. In more relevant information, you can request sample data here.
  • An outage geolocator / mapping device for visualizing ISI trinocular outage datasets over time. Future work includes generalizing for survey data and creating converters for different laboratories.
